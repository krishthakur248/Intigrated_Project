Accessibility Policy

Last Updated: April 20, 2025

We are committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

1. Our Commitment

We strive to ensure that our website is accessible to all users, regardless of ability or technology. Our goal is to meet or exceed the requirements of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA.

2. Accessibility Features

Our website includes the following accessibility features:

  • Text Alternatives: We provide text alternatives for non-text content.
  • Adaptable Content: Our content can be presented in different ways without losing information or structure.
  • Distinguishable Content: We make it easier for users to see and hear content, including separating foreground from background.
  • Keyboard Accessible: All functionality is available from a keyboard.
  • Time Adjustments: Users have enough time to read and use content.
  • Seizure Prevention: We do not design content in a way that is known to cause seizures.
  • Navigable Content: We provide ways to help users navigate, find content, and determine where they are.
  • Readable Content: We make text content readable and understandable.
  • Predictable Operation: Web pages appear and operate in predictable ways.
  • Input Assistance: We help users avoid and correct mistakes.
  • Compatible Technology: We maximize compatibility with current and future user tools.

3. Assistive Technology Compatibility

Our website is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers (such as JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, and TalkBack)
  • Text-to-speech software
  • Speech recognition software
  • Screen magnification software
  • Alternative keyboard and mouse inputs

4. What We're Doing to Improve Accessibility

We are continuously working to improve the accessibility of our website by:

  • Conducting regular accessibility audits
  • Training our staff on accessibility best practices
  • Involving users with disabilities in our testing processes
  • Keeping up to date with the latest accessibility technologies and standards

5. Known Accessibility Issues

Despite our best efforts, there may be some parts of our website that are not fully accessible. We are working to address these issues as quickly as possible. If you encounter any accessibility barriers, please contact us using the information below.
